About Me

I’m a software developer currently focused on developing for the Web because I am passionate about programming and the organization that comes up with the Web’s international standards, World Wide Web Consortium (W3C), aims to make the Web accessible to everyone and every device.

I believe that websites can fulfill my goal of making the lives of others better by helping them complete tasks easier and quicker so they can spend their time on things and people that truly matter to them. For example, Inferhythm helps get the lyrics from the website Genius for the track the user is currently listening to on Spotify with a couple of clicks.

Most of my experience is with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, but I have worked with MongoDB, Express, and Node.js. I’m willing to learn anything to solve problems or improve the product.

I recently graduated from Simon Fraser University's School of Interactive Arts and Technology (SIAT) with a Bachelor of Science.

When I'm not making websites, I enjoy playing basketball and video games, listening to music, or watching TV. I’m also developing a small mobile game with a friend for fun.
